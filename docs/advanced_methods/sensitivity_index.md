# Sensitivity-analysis index

We performed multiple sensitivity analyses. This page provides a compact index rather than a separate narrative page for each check.

| Question | Purpose | Primary figure |
|---|---|---|
| Do results depend on the RNA-sequencing resource? | Compare alternative tissue-expression resources | Supplementary Figures 6 and 8–11 |
| Do ancestry strata change the main pattern? | Evaluate population robustness | Supplementary Figure 6 |
| Do age strata change the main pattern? | Evaluate age-related robustness | Supplementary Figure 6 |
| Do sex strata change the main pattern? | Examine female and male profiles | Supplementary Figures 6 and 20–25, 32–37 |
| Do phenotype-measurement routes matter? | Compare fluid-intelligence assessment routes | Supplementary Figure 6 |
| Do correlated traits inflate pleiotropy? | Recalculate pleiotropy using a low-correlation trait subset | Supplementary Figure 7 |
| Do overlapping gene sets inflate comparisons? | Restrict to less-overlapping sets and independent traits | Supplementary Figure 12 |
| Are results driven by recurrent CNVs? | Remove CNVs carried by more than 20 participants | Supplementary Figure 13 |
| Are results driven by large CNVs? | Exclude CNVs encompassing five or more genes | Supplementary Figure 14 |
| Does carrier imbalance explain DEL-versus-DUP specificity? | Compare carrier ratios across significance categories | Supplementary Figure 15 |
| Do brain-related sets simply have more carriers? | Compare carriers in brain and non-brain gene sets | Supplementary Figure 16 |
| Do DEL and DUP hit different genes within sets? | Test whether gene overlap explains monotonicity | Supplementary Figure 46 |
