# License and permitted use

## Intended release policy

FunBurd is intended to be made available for **non-commercial research and educational use**. Commercial use requires separate written permission from the copyright holders.

Scholarly use of the FunBurd framework, code, documentation, or derived workflows should cite our accompanying publication:

> Kazem S, Kumar K, Yang J, Benitiere F, Huguet G, Mollon J, et al. **Determinants of functional burden pleiotropy and gene dosage responses across human traits.** medRxiv. Preprint version 3. https://www.medrxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.02.25.25322833v3

The final *Nature Communications* citation and DOI will replace the preprint citation after the article is published.

## Software code

The approved repository-level `LICENSE` file will govern software use after the final license has been selected by the copyright holders and the relevant institutional office.

The intended policy is to permit non-commercial research and educational use, modification, and redistribution subject to the approved notice and attribution requirements. Commercial use, commercial deployment, or incorporation into a commercial product or service requires separate written permission from the copyright holders.

## Documentation and original illustrations

Unless otherwise indicated, the original FunBurd documentation text and documentation-specific illustrations are intended for non-commercial reuse with attribution and citation of our accompanying publication.

Some manuscript panels and third-party visual components may be subject to separate rights or reuse conditions. Their inclusion in this documentation does not grant additional rights beyond those held by the copyright holders or permitted by the applicable source license.

## Citation requirement

When FunBurd is used in a publication, preprint, report, presentation, or public research output, cite our accompanying publication and link to the FunBurd repository. A versioned archive DOI will be added when available.

See [How to cite FunBurd](citation.md) for the preferred citation format.

## Questions about permitted use

For commercial licensing requests or questions about reuse beyond non-commercial research and education, contact the copyright holders using the contact information provided in our accompanying publication.
